The only problem is that he was brazen enough to actually disturb the swans to cause them to take flight. Those swans are rare in Ontario and are a regulated species. It is not allow to even 'disturb' those swans. So if anyone sees trumpeter swans, just leave them alone, or if you must, observe them cautiously and from a distance. You will know if you have disturbed them, because they will complaint by making some small noise, and looking at you. What is even worse is disturbing them while they are looking for a place to make there nest (springtime, now). From what I have read, there are only 3500 of those swans, or fewer.
